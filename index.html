Behavioral Science

Built on decades of
economic research.

Every design decision in Rethink is grounded in peer-reviewed behavioral economics literature.

Present Bias
We overvalue now.
Humans systematically overweight immediate rewards compared to future ones — making impulsive purchases feel right in the moment and regrettable minutes later. A brief pause rebalances this equation.
Laibson, D. (1997). Quarterly Journal of Economics.
Pain of Paying
Cash hurt. Tapping doesn't.
Paying with cash activates neural pain responses that curb spending. Digital payment has eliminated this friction entirely. Rethink reintroduces a cognitive equivalent of that pain.
Prelec & Loewenstein (1998). Marketing Science.
Temporal Friction
Time changes decisions.
Introducing a mandatory delay between impulse and action allows System 2 thinking to engage. Even a 10-second pause is enough to reduce impulsive choice rates significantly.
Frederick, Loewenstein & O'Donoghue (2002). JEL.
