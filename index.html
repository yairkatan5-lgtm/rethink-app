Behavioral Science

Not an opinion.
Decades of peer-reviewed research.

Every intervention Rethink delivers is grounded in how the human brain actually makes, and regrets, financial decisions.

L
L Loss Aversion
Losses sting twice as hard as gains feel good.
Kahneman & Tversky (1979) showed losses are felt approximately 2× more intensely than equivalent gains. Rethink reframes purchases as potential losses, "this costs 18 hours of your work", activating the brain's natural aversion to giving something up.
Kahneman & Tversky (1979). Prospect Theory. Econometrica.
M
M Mental Accounting
We bucket money, and it distorts decisions.
Thaler (1985) found people treat money differently depending on its mental "account." Rethink surfaces cumulative spending across categories, collapsing the illusion that each purchase is isolated, making the real total visible in real time.
Thaler, R. H. (1985). Mental Accounting and Consumer Choice. Marketing Science.
F
F Future-Self Continuity
We treat our future self like a stranger.
Hershfield et al. (2011) found that people with low future-self continuity spend more impulsively, they feel little connection to who they'll become. Rethink's "future-self bridge" messages make that future person feel real and worth protecting.
Hershfield et al. (2011). Increasing Saving Behavior. Journal of Marketing Research.
P
P Present Bias & Friction
Now feels urgent. Later feels abstract.
Present bias (Laibson, 1997) makes immediate rewards feel disproportionately valuable. Digital payments removed every natural speed bump. Rethink reintroduces temporal friction at the moment of purchase, giving the rational mind time to catch up.
Laibson (1997). Golden Eggs & Hyperbolic Discounting. QJE. · Prelec & Loewenstein (1998). Marketing Science.
COM-B Diagnosis

Why people overspend, and exactly where Rethink intervenes.

The COM-B model (Michie et al., 2011) maps behavior change across three components. Impulsive digital spending has a barrier in all three.

🧠 Capability
Psychological barrier
Low real-time awareness of spending patterns. Consumers lack the cognitive tools to connect individual transactions to their financial trajectory, each purchase feels small in isolation.
Rethink's fix: surfaces cumulative data at the moment of purchase.
🌍 Opportunity
Physical barrier
Frictionless UX removes every natural decision point. One-tap checkout, saved cards, and 24/7 availability collapse the gap between impulse and action to near zero.
Rethink's fix: mandatory pause reintroduces a structured decision window.
💡 Motivation
Automatic barrier
Dopamine-driven reward from purchasing overrides deliberate reasoning. Present bias ensures the future cost feels abstract while the immediate gratification feels concrete and urgent.
Rethink's fix: personalised AI nudge reframes the decision emotionally and rationally.

Questions

The things people ask
before they try it.

Will this slow down every purchase I make?
No. Rethink's AI distinguishes between routine purchases (groceries, bills, subscriptions) and discretionary ones that deviate from your pattern. You only feel friction when the data says you might regret it.
Can I override the pause and just pay?
Always. Rethink never blocks a payment, it creates a choice. You can complete any transaction at any time. The pause exists to make sure you're choosing, not just reacting.
Is it really free?
Yes, completely. Rethink doesn't charge you, doesn't sell your data, and doesn't take a cut of any payment. The only thing it costs you is 30 seconds.
How does the AI know my spending habits?
Rethink connects to your payment data (with your permission) and builds a personal baseline from your history. All processing is on-device. Your spending data never leaves your phone.
What if I use multiple payment methods?
Rethink works across payment methods and integrates with wallets, cards, and apps. The AI aggregates across sources so your insights are complete, not just what you paid with Apple Pay.
Is there research behind this approach?
Yes. Rethink is built on peer-reviewed behavioral economics research: the Pain of Paying (Prelec & Loewenstein), Present Bias (Laibson), and Temporal Friction (Frederick et al.). The science is real, we just built the app.
